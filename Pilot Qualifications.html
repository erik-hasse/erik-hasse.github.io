I. Preflight Preparation

A. Pilot Qualifications

Objective

To determine that the applicant exhibits satisfactory knowledge, risk management, and skills associated with airman and medical certificates including privileges, limitations, currency, and operating as pilot-in-command (PIC) as a private pilot.

Knowledge

  1. Certification requirements, recent flight experience, and recordkeeping
    • Certification requirements (61.103)
      • 17 years old
      • Read/write/speak/understand English
      • Medical certificate
      • Ground and flight endorsements
      • Meet the required aeronautical experience
      • Pass knowledge and flight tests
    • Recent flight experience:
      • Flight review - last 24 months (61.56)
        • Flight review and endorsement
        • Proficiency or practical test for a certificate or rating
      • Currency - last 90 days (61.57)
        • Cannot carry passengers unless current
        • 3 TOLs in the same category, class, and type
        • 3 night full stop TOLs to a full stop in the same category, class, and type (1 hour after sunset/before sunrise)
    • Recordkeeping (61.51)
      • Required for currency and meeting certificate/rating/flight review requirements
  2. Privileges and limitations (61.113):
    • May not pay less than pro-rata share limited to fuel, oil, airport fees, and rental fees
    • May not act as PIC for compensation or hire or carry passengers/property for compensation or hire
    • May act as PIC in these situations:
      • Incidental flight for business
      • Non-profit flights (see 91.146)
      • Reimbursed for search and rescue operations with government or organizations
      • Demo aircraft to buyers with at least 200 hours
      • Tow gliders (see 91.69)
      • Perform flight tests on light sport aircraft
  3. Medical certificates: class, expiration, privileges, temporary disqualifications (61.23)
    • Need at least a class 3 while exercising PPL privileges
    • Lasts 60 months under 40, 24 months over 40
    • Temporary disqualifications (61.53):
      • Any conditions, medication, or treatment that would make one ineligible for medical certificate
  4. Documents required to exercise private pilot privileges
    • Required documents (61.3)
      • Pilot certificate
      • Photo ID
      • Medical certificate
    • Additional endorsements (61.31)
      • Complex - fixed speed prop, retractable landing gear, and electric flaps
      • High performance - over 200 HP
      • Tailwheel
      • High altitude - pressurized
  5. Part 68 BasicMed privileges and limitations (68, 61.23(c)(3), 61.113(i))
    • Requirements:
      • Drivers license
      • Medical issued after July 14, 2006
      • Completed BasicMed Checklist
      • Regular Physical
      • Online Course
    • Limitations:
      • 6 total seats, 6000 lb MTOW, 5 passengers
      • 18000 MSL, 250 kts
      • Domestic only
      • No compensation or for hire
      • Must have info in logbook

Risk Management

  1. Failure to distinguish proficiency vs currency
    • Currency is a legal requirement, proficiency is the ability to do it safely
  2. Flying unfamiliar airplanes, or operating with unfamiliar flight display systems, and avionics
    • Receive training before doing anything unfamiliar

Skills

  1. Apply requirements to act as PIC under Visual Flight Rules (VFR) in a scenario given by the evaluator
