VI. Navigation

A. Pilotage and Dead Reckoning

Objective

To determine that the applicant exhibits satisfactory knowledge, risk management, and skills associated with pilotage and dead reckoning.

Knowledge

  1. Pilotage and dead reckoning.
  2. Magnetic compass errors.
  3. Topography.
  4. Selection of appropriate:
    1. Route
    2. Altitude(s)
    3. Checkpoints
  5. Plotting a course, to include:
    1. Determining heading, speed, and course
    2. Wind correction angle
    3. Estimating time, speed, and distance
    4. True airspeed and density altitude
  6. Power setting selection.
  7. Planned versus actual flight plan calculations and required corrections.

Risk Management

  1. Collision hazards, to include aircraft, terrain, obstacles, and wires.
  2. Distractions, loss of situational awareness, or improper task management.

Skills

  1. Prepare and use a flight log.
  2. Navigate by pilotage.
  3. Navigate by means of pre-computed headings, groundspeeds, and elapsed time.
  4. Use the magnetic direction indicator in navigation, to include turns to headings.
  5. Verify position within three nm of the flight-planned route.
  6. Arrive at the en route checkpoints within five minutes of the initial or revised estimated time of arrival (ETA) and provide a destination estimate.
  7. Maintain the appropriate altitude +-200 feet and heading +-15 deg.
