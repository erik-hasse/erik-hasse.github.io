III. Airport and Seaplane Base Operations

B. Traffic Patterns

Objective

To determine that the applicant exhibits satisfactory knowledge, risk management, and skills associated with traffic patterns.

Knowledge

  1. Towered and nontowered airport operations.
  2. Runway selection for the current conditions.
  3. Right-of-way rules.
  4. Use of automated weather and airport information.

Risk Management

  1. Collision hazards, to include aircraft, terrain, obstacles, and wires.
  2. Distractions, loss of situational awareness, or improper task management.
  3. Wake turbulence or windshear.

Skills

  1. Identify and interpret airport/seaplane base runways, taxiways, markings, signs, and lighting.
  2. Comply with recommended traffic pattern procedures.
  3. Correct for wind drift to maintain the proper ground track.
  4. Maintain orientation with the runway/landing area in use.
  5. Maintain traffic pattern altitude, +-100 feet, and the appropriate airspeed, +-10 knots.
  6. Maintain situational awareness and proper spacing from other aircraft in the traffic pattern.
