V. Performance and Ground Reference Maneuvers

A. Steep Turns

Objective

To determine that the applicant exhibits satisfactory knowledge, risk management, and skills associated with steep turns.

Knowledge

  1. Purpose of steep turns.
  2. Aerodynamics associated with steep turns, to include:
    1. Coordinated and uncoordinated flight
    2. Overbanking tendencies
    3. Maneuvering speed, including the impact of weight changes
    4. Load factor and accelerated stalls
    5. Rate and radius of turn

Risk Management

  1. Failure to divide attention between airplane control and orientation.
  2. Collision hazards, to include aircraft and terrain.
  3. Low altitude maneuvering including stall, spin, or CFIG.
  4. Distractions, improper task management, loss of situational awareness, or disorientation.
  5. Failure to maintain coordinated flight.

Skills

  1. Clear the area.
  2. Establish the manufacturer's recommended airspeed; or if one is not available, an airspeed not to exceed V_A.
  3. Roll into a coordinated 360 deg steep turn with approximately a 45 deg bank.
  4. Perform the Task in the opposite direction, as specified by evaluator.
  5. Maintain the entry altitude +-100 feet, airspeed +- 10 knots, bank +-5 deg, and roll out on the entry heading +-10 deg.
