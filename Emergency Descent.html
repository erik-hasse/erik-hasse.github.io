IX. Emergency Operations

A. Emergency Descent

Objective

To determine that the applicant exhibits satisfactory knowledge, risk management, and skills associated with an emergency descent.

Knowledge

  1. Situations that would require an emergency descent (e.g. depressurization, smoke, or engine fire).
  2. Immediate action items and emergency procedures:
    • Mixture out, fuel shutoff valve out
    • Tight spiral descent
  3. Airspeed, to include airspeed limitations
    • Vne - 163 kts
    • Vno - 129 kts

Risk Management

  1. Failure to consider altitude, wind, terrain, obstructions, and available glide distance.
  2. Collision hazards, to include aircraft, terrain, obstacles, and wires.
  3. Improper airplane configuration.
  4. Distractions, loss of situational awareness, or improper task management.

Skills

  1. Clear the area.
  2. Establish and maintain the appropriate airspeed and configuration appropriate to the scenario specified by the evaluator and as covered in the POH/AFM for the emergency descent.
  3. Maintain orientation, divide attention appropriately, and plan and execute a smooth recovery.
  4. Use bank angle between 30 deg and 45 deg to maintain positive load factors during the descent.
  5. Maintain appropriate airspeed +0/-10 knots, and level off at a specified altitude +-100 feet.
  6. Complete the appropriate checklist.
