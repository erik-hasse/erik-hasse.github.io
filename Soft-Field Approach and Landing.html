IV. Takeoffs, Landings, and Go-Arounds

D. Soft-Field Approach and Landing

Objective

To determine that the applicant exhibits satisfactory knowledge, risk management, and skills associated with a soft-field approach and landing with emphasis on proper use of flight controls.

Knowledge

  1. A stabilized approach, to include energy management concepts.
  2. Effects of atmospheric conditions, including wind, on approach and landing performance.
  3. Wind correction techniques on approach and landing.

Risk Management

  1. Selection of runway or approach path and touchdown area based on pilot capability, airplane performance and limitations, available distance, and wind.
  2. Effects of:
    1. Crosswind
    2. Windshear
    3. Tailwind
    4. Wake turbulence
    5. Runway surface/condition
  3. Planning for:
    1. Go-around and rejected landing
    2. Land and hold short operations (LAHSO)
  4. Collision hazards, to include aircraft, terrain, obstacles, wires, vehicles, vessels, persons, and wildlife.
  5. Low altitude maneuvering including stall, spin, or CFIT.
  6. Distractions, loss of situational awareness, or improper task management.

Skills

  1. Complete the appropriate checklist.
  2. Make radio calls as appropriate.
  3. Ensure the airplane is aligned with the correct/assigned runway or landing surface.
  4. Scan the runway or landing surface and the adjoining area for traffic or obstructions.
  5. Select and aim for a suitable touchdown point considering the wind, landing surface, and obstructions.
  6. Establish the recommended approach and landing configuration and airspeed, and adjust pitch attitude and power as required to maintain a stabilized approach.
  7. Maintain manufacturer's published approach airspeed or in its absence not more than 1.3V_SO, +10/-5 knots with gust factor applied.
  8. Maintain directional control and appropriate crosswind correction throughout the approach and landing.
  9. Make smooth, timely, and correct control inputs during round out and touchdown, and, for tricycle gear airplanes, keep the nosewheel off the suface until loss of elevator effectiveness
  10. Touch down at a proper pitch attitude with minimum sink rate, no side drift, and with the airplane's longitudinal axis aligned with the center of the runway.
  11. Maintain elevator as recommended by manufacturer during rollout and exit the "soft" area at a speed that would preclude sinking into the surface.
  12. Execute a timely go-around if the approach cannot be made within the tolerances specified above or for any other condition that may result in an unsafe approach or landing.
  13. Maintain proper position of the flight controls and sufficient speed to taxi while on the soft surface.
