I. Preflight Preparation

G. Operation of Systems

Objective

To determine that the applicant exhibits satisfactory knowedge, risk management, and skills associated with the safe operation of systems on the airplane provided for the flight test.

Knowledge

  1. Airplane systems, to include:
    1. Primary flight controls
    2. Secondary flight controls
    3. Powerplant and propeller
      • Lycoming IO-360 at 180 HP
      • Horizontally opposed, 4 cylinder, air cooled, fuel injected
    4. Landing gear
    5. Fuel, oil, and hydraulic
      • Fuel:
        • 56 Gal, 53 useable (28/26.5 per tank)
        • Vent under left wing
        • 100 octane, low lead, blue
        • 5 sumps on each wing, plus 3 under engine
      • Oil:
        • 9 quarts, 8 useable, 5 or 6 minimum
        • 7.5 lbs/gallon
        • Max temp: 245 deg F, max pressure: 20 PSI
        • Purposes:
          • Lubricates - reduces wear/friction heat
          • Cools - carries away heat
          • Seals
          • Cleans
      • Red MIL-H-5606 hydraulic fluid
    6. Electrical
      • 28 V, DC system
      • 60 Amp alternator
      • 24 V battery
      • Systems are protected by circuit breakers
      • Systems:
        • Radio
        • Turn coordinator
        • Fuel gauges
        • Pitot heat
        • Lights
        • Flaps
        • Oil temp gauge
        • Auxiliary fuel pump
    7. Avionics
    8. Pitot-static, vacuum/pressure, and associated flight instruments
      • Pitot-static
        • Altimeter - pressure difference from reference
        • Vertical speed - differential pressure with calibrated vent
        • Airspeed
          • differential pressure between pitot tube and static source
          • Types:
            • Indicated - what's shown on the airspeed indicator
            • Calibrated - indicated corrected for position or instrument errors
            • Equivalent - indicated corrected for position, instrument, and compressible flow
            • True - calibrated corrected for altitude and temperature
          • Green arc - takeoff/cruise
          • White arc - flaps/landing
      • Vac/pressure:
        • Attitude - rigidity in space, limited operational angles
        • Heading - rigidity in space, precession errors, earth rotation errors
        • Turn coordinator - precession
    9. Environmental
    10. Deicing and anti-icing
    11. N/A
    12. Oxygen system
      • Crew - Required if between 12500 MSL to 14000 MSL for more than 30 minutes
      • Crew - Required any time above 14000 MSL
      • Provide to passengers above 15000 MSL
  2. Indications of and procedures for managing system abnormalities or failures

Risk Management

  1. Failure to detect system malfunctions or failures.
  2. Improper management of a system failure.
  3. Failure to monitor and manage automated systems.

Skills

  1. Operate at least three of the systesm listed in K1a through K1l above appropriately.
  2. Use appropirate checklists properly.
