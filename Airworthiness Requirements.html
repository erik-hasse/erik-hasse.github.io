I. Preflight Preparation

B. Airworthiness Requirements

Objective

To determine that the applicant exhibits satisfactory knowlege, risk management, and skills associated with airworthiness requirements, including airplane certificates.

Knowledge

  1. General airworthiness requirements and compliance for airplanes, including:
    1. Certificate location and expiration dates (91.203)
      • *A*irworthiness certificate - On display to passengers and crew, no expiration date
      • *R*egistration - On plane, valid for 3 years
      • *R*adio station license - Outside US only
      • *O*perating Handbook - Specific to serial number (91.9)
      • *W*eights and balance
      • Compass deviation card
      • External Data Plate/Serial Number
    2. Required inspections and airplane logbook documentation
      • Required inspections:
        • *A*irworthiness directives (91.403)
        • *V*OR check (91.171) - 30 days, IFR only
        • *I*nspections - annual and (if for hire) 100 hour (91.409)
        • *A*ltimeter (91.411) - 24 months, IFR only
        • *T*ransponder (91.413) - 24 months
        • *E*LT (91.207) - 12 months
        • *S*tatic System (91.411) - 24 months, IFR only
      • Records (91.417):
        • Maintenance, preventive maintenance, alterations, or inspections:
          • Description of work
          • Date
          • Signature & certificate number
        • Other records:
          • Total airframe, engine, propeller times
          • Time since overhaul
          • Time in service for life limited parts
          • AD status
    3. Airworthiness directives and Special Airworthiness Information Bulletins
      • ADs
        • Either emergency or less urgent
        • Must be complied with
      • SAIBs
        • Generally alerts and recommendations
        • Non-regulatory
    4. Purpose and procedure for obtaining a special flight permit
      • Obtained to fly a safe but technically non-airworthy aircraft
      • Contact FSDO
  2. Pilot-performed preventive maintenance
    • Pilots may perform preventive maintenance (43.3(g)) & approve for return to service (43.7(f))
    • Must log name, date, description, and signature (43.9)
    • See 43 Appendix A(c) for allowed maintenance
    • Common items:
      • Tires
      • Lubrication
      • Hydraulic fluid
      • Bulbs of position and landing lights
      • Spark plugs
      • Batteries
  3. Equipment requirements for day and night VFR flight, to include:
    • Day VFR (91.205(b)):
      • *A*irspeed indicator
      • *T*achometer
      • *O*il pressure gauge
      • *M*anifold pressure gauge
      • *A*ltitude indicator
      • *T*emperature gauge (liquid cooled)
      • *O*il temperature gauge (air cooled)
      • *F*uel gauge
      • *F*lotation gear - if over water for hire
      • *L*anding gear indicator
      • *A*nticollision lights
      • *M*agnetic direction indicator
      • *E*LT
      • *S*afety belts
    • Night VFR (91.205(c)):
      • *F*uses
      • *L*anding lights (for hire only)
      • *A*nticollision lights
      • *P*osition lights
      • *S*ource of electricity
    • Flying with inoperative equipment
      • *R*egulations - ATOMATOFLAMES/FLAPS
      • *A*irworthiness directive
      • *K*inds of operating equipment list - 141 flight school or 135 op
      • *E*quipment list (POH)
      • *T*ype certificate
      • *S*afety of flight
    • Using an approved Minimum Equipment List (MEL)
      • Type certificate/AD if not in MEL
      • Consult MEL for minimum required of each item
    • Kinds of Operation Equipment List (KOEL)
      • Usually only present in a part 141 flight school or a 135 op
    • Required discrepancy records or placards
      • Remove, placard, and log it
      • Deactivate and placard

Risk Management

  1. Inoperative equipment discovered prior to flight

Skills

  1. Locate and describe airplane airworthiness and registration information
  2. Determine the airplane is airworthy in a scenario given by the evaluator
  3. Apply appropriate procedures for operating with inoperative equipment in a scenario given by the evaluator
