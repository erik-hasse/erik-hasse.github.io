I. Preflight Preparation

H. Human Factors

Objective

To determine that the applicant exhibits satisfactory knowedge, risk management, and skills associated with personal health, flight physiology, aeromedical and human facotrs, as it relates to safety of flight

Knowledge

  1. The symptoms (as applicable), recognition, causes, effects, and corrective actions associated with aeromedical and physiological issues including:
    1. Hypoxia
      • Insufficient oxygen which impairs brain and other organs
      • Types:
        • Hypoxic - Air
        • Hypemic - Blood
        • Stagnant - Heart
        • Histotoxic - Cells
      • Any altitude, but generally above 12,000 feet
      • Use oxygen, avoid breathing fumes
      • Symptoms:
        • Impaired memory, alterness, coordination, judgement
        • Headache
        • Drowsy/dizzy
        • Sense of well-being or belligerence
    2. Hyperventilation
      • Abnormal increase in breath volume
      • Decreased blood CO2
      • Talk or breath into bag, control breath
      • Symptoms:
        • Light headedness
        • Suffocation
        • Drowsiness
        • Tingling
        • Coolness
    3. Middle ear and sinus problems
      • Ear block is when inner ear pressure can't equalize
      • Yawn, swallow, tense throat muscles, pinch nose and try to blow through it
      • Most common with allergies, a cold, or other congestion
    4. Spatial disorientation
      • *I*nversion Illusion - level-off after sustained climb/descent
      • *C*oriolis Illusion - Head movements
      • *E*levator Illusion - Up and down drafts
      • *F*alse horizon - sloping terrain, lights, clouds
      • *L*eans - banking or turning sensation
      • *A*utokinesis - stationary lights appear to move
      • *G*raveyard spiral - descending turn but feels level
      • *G*raveyard spin - After recovering from a spin, pilot feels like they're in a new spin
      • *S*omotogravic Illusion - Acceleration & deceleration feels like pitching
    5. Motion sickness
      • Continued simulation of the inner ear
      • Open air vents, loosen clothing, look outside
      • Symptoms:
        • Loss of appetite
        • Excess saliva
        • Perspiration
        • Nausea
        • Disorientation
        • Headached
        • Vomitting
    6. Carbon monoxide poisoning
      • Most commonly caused by cabin heat problem
      • Shut heat off, open windows, land
      • Symptoms similar to hypoxia
    7. Stress
    8. Fatigue
    9. Dehydration and nutrition
    10. Hypothermia
    11. Optical illusions
    12. Dissolved nitrogen in the bloodstream after scuba dives
      • Below 8000 ft:
        • 12 hours after any dive
        • 24 hours after controlled ascent dive
      • Above 8000 ft:
        • 24 hours after any dive
  2. Regulations regarding use of alcohol and drugs.
    • No alcohol within 8 hours and 0.04 BAC
    • In general, don't fly on medication unless it's OKed by the FAA/an AME
  3. Effects of alcohol, drugs, and over-the-counter medications.
  4. Aeronautical Decision-Making.
    • Single-Pilot Resource Management (SRM):
      • Use all the resources available to optimize outcome
      • 5 Ps:
        • Plan
        • Plane
        • Pilot
        • Passengers
        • Programming
    • ADM is a systemic approach to determine the best course of action
    • DECIDE model:
      • *D*etect a change
      • *E*stimate the need to counter or react to it
      • *C*hoose the most desirable outcome
      • *I*dentify actions to control it
      • *D*o something to adapt
      • *E*valuate the effect of the action
    • 3 Ps:
      • Perceive
        • *P*ilot
        • *A*ircraft
        • en*V*ironment
        • *E*xternal pressures
      • Process
        • *C*onsequences
        • *A*lternatives
        • *R*eality
        • *E*xternal pressures
      • Perform
        • *T*ransfer
        • *E*liminate
        • *A*ccept
        • *M*itigate
    • I'M SAFE:
      • *I*llness
      • *M*edication
      • *S*tress
      • *A*lcohol
      • *F*atigue
      • *E*motions

Risk Management

  1. Aeromedical and physiological issues.
  2. Hazardous attitudes.
    • *M*acho - Taking chances is foolish
    • *A*nti-Authority - Follow the rules, they are usually right
    • *R*esignation - I can make a difference, I am not helpless
    • *I*mpulsivity - Think first, not so fast
    • *I*nvulnerability - It could happen to me
  3. Distractions, loss of situational awareness, or improper task management.

Skills

  1. Associate the symptoms and effects for at least three of the conditions listed in K1a through K1l above with the cause(s) and corrective actions(s).
  2. Perform self-assessment, including fitness for flight and personal minimums, for actual flight or a scenario given by the evaluator.
