I. Preflight Preparation

F. Performance and Limitations

Objective

To determine that the applicant exhibits satisfactory knowedge, risk management, and skills associated with operating an airplane safely within the parameters of its performance capabilities and limitations.

Knowledge

  1. Elements related to performance and limitations by explaining the use of charts, tables, and data to determine performance.
    • Elements:
      • Takeoff and landing distance
      • Rate of climb, Ceiling
      • Payload
      • Range, speed, fuel economy
      • Maneuverability, stability
  2. Factors affecting performance, to include:
    1. Atmospheric conditions
      • Density altitude
        • Pressure altitude corrected for temperature
        • Affected by altitude, temperature, and humidity
        • Less lift from wings, lower engine power, less propeller efficiency, less drag
      • Surface wind
    2. Pilot technique
    3. Airplane configuration
      • weight
    4. Airport environment
      • Runway surface
      • Runway slope
    5. Loading (e.g. center of gravity)
      • Overloaded:
        • Higher TO speed/longer TO run
        • Reduced rate & angle of climb
        • Lower max altitude
        • Shorter range
        • Reduced speed, maneuverability
        • Higher stalling speed
        • Higher landing speed/longer landing roll
        • Excessive nosewheel weight
      • Forward CG:
        • Higher stall speed
        • Slower cruise speed
        • More stable
        • Greater back elevator needed
      • Aft CG:
        • Lower stall speed
        • Higher cruise speed
        • Less stable
    6. Weight and balance
      • Empty weight - airframe, engins, permanent equipment, unusable fuel, full oil (only unusable oil on some planes)
      • Gross weight - maximum allowable weight
      • Useful load - Gross weight-empty weight
      • Arm - distance from reference to CG
      • Moment - Arm*weight
      • Center of gravity - Balance point
      • Datum - reference plane
  3. Aerodynamics.
    • Angle of incidence - angle between longitudinal axis & wing chord
    • Items affecting lift and drag:
      • Wing area
      • Shape of airfoil
      • Angle of attack
      • Velocity of air
      • Air density
    • Torque effect:
      • Torque reaction of engine & propeller - rolls plane to left
      • Gyroscopic precession - Force applied 90 deg ahead in rotation
      • Slipstream corkscrew - yaw left
      • P-factor - high AOA means the downward blade has larger bite than upward blade -> yaw left
    • Load factor
      • Force / weight
      • 1/cos(bank angle) in level turn
      • Stall speed proportional to sqrt(load factor)
    • Maneuvering speed
      • Max speed where plane stalls before structural damage.
      • Maneuvering speed increases with increase in weight
    • Adverse yaw - extra drag on downward deflected aileron (upward wing) causes yaw
    • Ground effect
      • Reduced drag when near the ground
      • Extra float on landing
      • Plane may seem ready to fly before sufficient airspeed

Risk Management

  1. Inaccurate use of manufacturer's performance charts, tables, and data.
  2. Exceeding airplane limitations.
  3. Possible differences between calculated performance and actual performance.

Skills

  1. Compute the weight and balance, correct out-of-center of gravity (CG) loading errors and determine if the weight and balance remains within limits during all phases of flight.
  2. Utilize the appropriate airplane manufacturer's approved performance charts, tables, and data.
