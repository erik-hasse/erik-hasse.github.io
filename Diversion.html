VI. Navigation

C. Diversion

Objective

To determine that the applicant exhibits satisfactory knowledge, risk management, and skills associated with diversion.

Knowledge

  1. Selecting an alternate destination.
  2. Situations that require deviations from flight plan or ATC instructions.

Risk Management

  1. Collision hazards, to include aircraft, terrain, obstacles, and wires.
  2. Distractions, loss of situational awareness, or improper task management.
  3. Failure to make a timely decision to divert.
  4. Failure to select an appropriate airport or seaplane base.
  5. Failure to utilize all available resources (e.g. automation, ATC, and flight deck planning aids).

Skills

  1. Select a suitable destination and route for diversion.
  2. Make a reasonable estimate of heading, groundspeed, arrival time, and fuel consumption to the divert airport.
  3. Maintain the appropriate altitude +-200 feet and heading +-15 deg.
  4. Update/interpret weather in flight.
  5. Utilize flight deck displays of digital weather and aeronautical information, as applicable.
