IX. Emergency Operations

B. Emergency Approach and Landing (Simulated)

Objective

To determine that the applicant exhibits satisfactory knowledge, risk management, and skills associated with emergency approach and landing procedures.

Knowledge

  1. Immediate action items and emergency procedures.
    • Airspeed (68 kts)
    • Best landing area
    • Checklist (restart the engine)
      • Fuel selector/shutoff
      • Mixture/throttle
      • Ignition/fuel pump
      • Declare
  2. Airspeed, to include:
    1. Importance of best glide speed and its relationship to distance
    2. Difference between best glide speed and minimum sink speed
    3. Effects of wind on glide distance
  3. Effects of atmospheric conditions on emergency approach and landing.
  4. A stabilized approch, to include energy management concepts.
  5. ELTs and other emergency locating devices.
  6. ATC services to aircraft in distress.

Risk Management

  1. Failure to consider altitude, wind, terrain, obstructions, gliding distance and available landing distance.
  2. Failure to plan and follow a flightpath to the selected landing area.
  3. Collision hazards, to include aircraft, terrain, obstacles, and wires.
  4. Improper airplane configuration.
  5. Low altitude maneuvering including stall, spin, or CFIT.
  6. Distractions, loss of situational awareness, or improper task management.

Skills

  1. Establish and maintain the best glide airspeed, +-10 knots.
  2. Configure the airplane in accordance with the POH/AFM and existing conditions.
  3. Select a suitable landing area considering altitude, wind, terrain, obstructions, and available glide distance.
  4. Plan and follow a flightpath to the selected landing area considering altitude, wind, terrain, and obstructions.
  5. Prepare for landing as specified by the evaluator.
  6. Complete the appropriate checklist.
