I. Preflight Preparation

E. National Airspace System

Objective

To determine that the applicant exhibits satisfactory knowedge, risk management, and skills associated with the National Airspace System (NAS) operating under VFR as a private pilot.

Knowledge

  1. Types of airspace/airspace classes and associated requirements and limitations (71)
    • Class A:
      • 18,000 to FL600
      • IFR only
    • Class B:
      • Busiest airports, generally 3 tiers, to 10,000 MSL, and 30 nm
      • 3 mi vis, clear of clouds
      • Radio, mode C transponder, ADS-B out
      • Explicit clearance to enter
      • Solid blue lines
    • Class C:
      • Busy airports, generally 2 tiers, to 4000 AGL, and 10 nm
      • 3 mi vis, 1000 ft above, 500 ft below, 2000 ft horizontal
      • Radio, mode C transponder, ADS-B out
      • Establish 2 way communication
      • Solid magenta lines
    • Class D:
      • Less busy airports, generally to 2500 AGL and 3-5 nm
      • 3 mi vis, 1000 ft above, 500 ft below, 2000 ft horizontal
      • Radio
      • Establish 2 way communication
      • Dashed blue lines
    • Class E:
      • Controlled other than A-D, one of the following:
        • *S*urface - dashed magenta lines
        • *E*xtension - dashed magenta lines
        • *T*ransition - 700 AGL, faded magenta lines
        • *V*ictor airways - lies connecting VORs
        • *O*ffshore - Blue zipper lines
        • *D*omestic enroute - anywhere else 1200 AGL to 17,999
        • *A*bove 14,500 (and above class A)
      • Below 10,000 MSL: 3 mi vis, 1000 ft above, 500 ft below, 2000 ft horizontal
      • At or above 10,000 MSL: 5 mi vis, 1000 ft above, 1000 ft below, 1 mi horizontal
      • No equipment or communication requirements
    • Class G:
      • Uncontrolled airspace, smaller airports or near the surface
      • Day, at or below 1200 AGL: 1 mi vis, clear of clouds
      • Day, above 1200 AGL and less than 10,000 MSL: 1 mi vis, 1000 ft above, 500 ft below, 2000 ft horizontal
      • Night, below 10,000 MSL: 3 mi vis, 1000 ft above, 500 ft below, 2000 ft horizontal
      • Above 1200 AGL and at or above 10,000 MSL: 5 mi vis, 1000 ft above, 1000 ft below, 1 mi horizontal
      • No equipment or communication requirements
  2. Charting symbology
  3. Special use airspace (SUA), special flight rules area (SFRA), temporary flight restrictions (TFR), and other airspace areas (73)
    • *M*ilitary operations area - Permission not required
    • *C*ontrolled firing area - Not labeled, fire automatically stopped
    • *P*rohibited area - Never enter
    • *R*estricted area - Requires permission from controlling agency
    • *A*lert area - Permission not required
    • *W*arning area - international waters
    • *N*ational Security/TFR - Never enter, check NOTAMs
    • TRSA - class D with approach control, gray lines
