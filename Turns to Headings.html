VIII. Basic Instrument Manuevers

D. Turns to Headings

Objective

To determine that the applicant exhibits satisfactory knowledge, risk management, and skills associated with attitude instrument flying during turns to headings solely by reference to instruments.

Knowledge

  1. Flight instruments as related to:
    1. Sensitivity, limitations, and potential errors in unusual attitudes
    2. Correlation (pitch instruments/bank instruments)
    3. Function and operation
    4. Proper instrument cross-check techniques

Risk Management

  1. Instrument flying hazards to include failure to maintain VFR, spatial disorientation, loss of control, fatigue, stree, and emergency off airport landings.
  2. Failure to seek assistance or declare an emergency in a deteriorating situation.
  3. Collision hazards, to include aircraft, terrain, obstacles, and wires.
  4. Distractions, loss of situational awareness, or improper task management.

Skills

  1. Turn to headings, maintain altitude +- 200 feet, maintain a standard rate turn, roll out on the assigned heading +-10 deg, and maintain airspeed +-10 knots.
