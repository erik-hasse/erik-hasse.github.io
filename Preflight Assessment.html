II. Preflight Procedures

A. Preflight Assessment

Objective

To determine that the applicant exhibits satisfactory knowledge, risk management, and skills associated with preparing for safe flight.

Knowledge

  1. Pilot self-assessment.
  2. Determining that the airplane to be used is appropriate and airworthy.
  3. Airplane preflight inspection including:
    1. Which items must be inspected
    2. The reasons for checking each item
    3. How to detect possible defects
    4. The associated regulations
  4. Environmental factors including weather, terrain, route selection, and obstructions.

Risk Management

  1. Pilot.
  2. Aircraft.
  3. Environment (e.g. weather, airports, airspace, terrain, obstacles).
  4. External pressures.
  5. Aviation security concerns.

Skills

  1. Inspect the airplane with reference to an appropriate checklist.
  2. Verify the airplane is in condition for safe flight and conforms to its type design.
