IX. Emergency Operations

C. Systems and Equipment Malfunction

Objective

To determine that the applicant exhibits satisfactory knowledge, risk management, and skills associated with system and equipment malfunctions appropriate to the airplane provided for the practical test and analyzing the situation and take appropriate action for simulated emergencies.

Knowledge

  1. Partial or complete power loss related to the specific powerplant, including:
    1. Engin roughness or overheat
    2. Carburator or induction icing
    3. Loss of oil pressure
    4. Fuel starvation
  2. System and equipment malfunctions specific to the airplane, including:
    1. Electrical malfunction
    2. Vacuum/pressure and associated flight instrument malfunctions
    3. Pitot/static system malfunction
    4. Electronic flight deeck display malfunction
    5. Landing gear or flap malfunction
    6. Inoperative trim
  3. Smoke/fire/engine compartment fire.
  4. Any other system specific to the airplane (e.g. supplemental oxygen, deicing).
  5. Inadvertent door or window opening.

Risk Management

  1. Failure to use the proper checklist for a system or equipment malfunction.
  2. Distractions, loss of situational awareness, or improper task mangement.

Skills

  1. Describe the appropriate action for simulated emergencies specified by the evaluator, from at least three of the elements or sub-elements listed in K1-K5 above.
  2. Complete the appropriate checklist.
